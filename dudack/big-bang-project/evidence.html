Evidence One

Redshift & Hubble's Law

So basically imagine you're standing on the sidewalk and an ambulance drives past you. The siren sounds higher when its coming towards you and lower when its driving away. Thats called the Doppler effect and it actually works with light too not just sound.

When a galaxy is moving away from us its light gets stretched out and shifts toward red. And the farther away a galaxy is the faster its moving. This guy Edwin Hubble figured that out in 1929 and basically realized the whole universe is expanding. If you run it backwards everything traces back to one single point which is kind of crazy.

02
Evidence Two

Cosmic Microwave Background

So in 1965 these two scientists named Penzias and Wilson kept hearing this really annoying hissing noise in their antenna and couldn't figure out where it was coming from. They tried cleaning it and everything. There were actually pigeons living inside the antenna so they got rid of those too. But it just kept hissing no matter what they did.

Turns out the hiss was coming from literally every direction in the sky all at once. It was the oldest light in the entire universe which is pretty insane. Its basically the leftover glow from the Big Bang that has been cooling down for 13.8 billion years and now its only like 2.725 degrees above absolute zero. They ended up winning the Nobel Prize for finding it.

Evidence Three

Element Abundance

So the Big Bang theory made this really specific prediction way before anyone could even test it. It said the early universe should have made around 75% hydrogen and 25% helium and basically nothing else.

And then when scientists actually went and measured the oldest gas clouds in space, like clouds that havent been changed by any stars yet, they found exactly that ratio every single time. No other theory has ever predicted something that specific and then been right about it. I think thats what makes this one so convincing.

Evidence Four
E = mc²

E = mc²

Albert Einstein came up with this equation in 1905 when he was working on special relativity. E stands for energy, m stands for mass, and c is the speed of light. When you square the speed of light you get a really big number so even a tiny amount of mass has like a huge amount of energy inside it. And it goes the other way too where enough energy can actually turn into mass.

In the very beginning of the universe it was so hot that energy kept turning into particles but they always came out in pairs, one matter and one antimatter, and they would just destroy each other right away. This happened over and over basically nonstop.

But there was this tiny imbalance where there was just slightly more matter than antimatter. Like for every billion antimatter particles there was one extra matter particle. That one extra is literally every atom that exists. Every star, every planet, everything. Pretty crazy when you actually think about it.
